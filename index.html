Web Development

In March of 2022, I joined forces with a fellow student to attempt my first Hackathon. We ended up creating Colorado Photoshoot Location Finder, a functional proof of concept for a site that allows photographers to find shoot locations based on images that visually inspire them. The images are tagged with descriptors relating to qualities such as mood, ambience, ease of access, and general lighting conditions. I envisioned a way to crowdsource and distribute the collective knowledge of local photographers.

I designed and implemented the UI/UX and integrated Google Maps into our site. We ended up winning Best UI/UX overall. Not bad for a first showing!

Check it out on Devpost here, or view as a live site here.

Other little projects I've done include Nail Polish Name Generator and Terrible Phone Number Input.

My coursework has also given me experience in Bootstrap, PostgreSQL, Docker, NodeJS, and EJS. For creating wireframes, I use Figma and Adobe XD.

Graphic Design

❮ ❯

The prompt for this project was to "design packaging for a food product that captures your personality." As a lover of spicy food, I knew I had to design a hot sauce. I wanted the product to come across as fun, friendly, humorous, and as a gentle introduction for those who may be new to hot sauce.

The background pattern, layout, and graphics are all original and created in Adobe Illustrator. The 3D product render was created in Adobe Dimension, featuring my actual kitchen countertop. All copy is original as well.

I was asked to shoot product photos for a local jewelry maker. I thought the photos turned out really well, so I took the liberty of turning them into ads. I tried to mirror and enhance the earthy, peaceful atmosphere of the photographs, as well as the dainty, feminine look of the jewelry.

All layouts and text design were created using Canva. All copy is original.

Photography

I started shooting seriously in 2019, my freshman year of college. I've since shot for local clothing stores (Apocalypse and Common Threads), restaurants, and Denver Fashion Week, as well as a wide variety of senior/grad photoshoots and personal branding shoots. Through it all, my true love remains the same: fashion and portrait photography. All my shooting and editing skills are self taught.

My work has been published in 303 Magazine! Check that out and more at my photography website.

I currently shoot with my trusty Nikon D750 paired with a 50mm prime lens, as well as film cameras when the mood strikes (Nikon FM, Canon AE-1). I do most of my editing in DarkTable.
